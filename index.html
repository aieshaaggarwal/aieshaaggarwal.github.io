Foraging Behaviour of Convict Cichlids Under Predation

Behaviour Ecology, University of Western Ontario, 2015

I took on the role of project leader as my research partner and I designed and carried out an experiment to investigate correlations in fish feeding behaviour and energetic costs and percieved predation risks. I took initiative in creating a log sheet to ensure the data were well organized, then transferred the raw data to an excel spreadsheet to perform the statistical analyses using R. Figures were also created using R. research paper writing and presentation. The results were presented in a scientific research paper. A presentation was given to the class using enthusiasm, visual diagrams and figures to explain our results in a logic and captivating manner.

Key Words: Optimal foraging theory, marginal value theorem, experimental design, R, statistics, Excel, PowerPoint, data collection, scientific writing, scientific communication

3 Methods to Predict Lake Morphometrics

A Fleming College Collaborative Project for the Ministry of Natural Resources and Forestry

My collaborative group consulted the MNRF in viable methods to predict lake maximum and mean depths for Ontario's lakes using Python programming. As head of visuals I created cartographically pleasing and informative maps and diagrams to convey the groups findings. I also assisted with data pre-processing, report writing and Python programming.

Key Words: ArcGIS Pro, pre-processing, cartography, Adobe Illustrator, Python programming, innovation, report writing, bathymetry

Copeland Forest Ecology Guide

A project for The Couchiching Conservancy & Copeland Forest Friends Association

Performed a ground truth study to confirm the location of species at risk and the invasive species. Compiled a guiding document for Copeland Forest Friends Ecology and Sustainability Committee with maps, background information, monitoring techniques and sample data collection sheets for future citizen science monitoring projects

Key Words: conservation, species identification, mapping, citizen science, GPS, GIS

Using GIS to Model an Optimal Cooridor for the Grey Wolf

Spatial Analysis, Fleming College, 2017

To determine the path Grey Wolves could travel through Yellowstone National Park to minimize energetic costs. Background research on the habitat preferences of grey wolves were used to select appropriate criteria to include in the analysis. A Least cost path analysis was performed in ArcMap 10.4. The final map was designed in ArcGIS Pro

Key Words: spatial analysis, gis, cartography, weighted sum, least cost path, raster analyses, euclidean distance
